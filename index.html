Hi, I'm Sahitya.

I like applying math, computer science, and statistics to a (very) wide spectrum of applications, including but not limited to: natural language understanding, clean energy, financial markets, nuclear non-proliferation, astrophysics, genomics, and recommendation systems.

I am currently a Machine Learning Engineer at Spotify, working on platform personalization, based in New York, NY.

Previously, I was a Data Scientist at Microsoft working on NLP applications. I graduated in 2019 from Stanford University with an M.S. at the Institute for Computational and Mathematical Engineering, and in 2017, I graduated from Cornell University with a B.S. in Computer Science.

Check out my resume or some projects below.

A few projects I've worked on

I worked with the Search, Assistant, and Intelligence team at Microsoft to inject user-personalization into Outlook SmartCompose, detailed in this ACL 2020 publication.

I worked with the Machine Learning group at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory on object detection and tracking using convolutional recurrent attention models and optical flow for video representation learning in multi-modal models.

With Computational Neuroscience faculty at Stanford, I created and implemented a system to detect and remove biases in brain MRI using adversarial architectures, detailed in this paper.

I performed statistical genomics research with the Statistics department at Stanford, developing methods for large-scale genome wide association studies, featured in this publication.

I researched with Astrophysics and Radiophysics at Cornell, analyzing infrared images of the center of the Milky Way Galaxy to understand star formation and dust gathering in the area.
